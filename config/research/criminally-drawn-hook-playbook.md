## 2. Don't Look At Me — what to steal (and what NOT to)

Channel: @dontlookatmeYT (~359K subs, 56M+ views). True crime + unsolved mysteries, human narration, iceberg compilations.

### Steal these patterns

**Title voice** — emotional incompleteness, spoken reaction, promise of escalation:
- "this case just gets worse and worse..."
- "one of the stranger cases i've covered..."
- "yogurt shop cold case finally solved..."
- "how many mistakes can you make..."
- "2 disturbing cases that were solved..."

Shape: **[reaction or paradox]. [optional case anchor].** Lowercase casual is their brand; Criminally Drawn should use **sentence case + dry precision** instead of "this guy is insane."

**Thumbnail logic** (from their CTR + industry benchmarks):
- One hero subject, phone-readable at small size
- 3–4 words max on overlay (added in post, not in Flow image)
- Formulas that work: timeline ("17 Years. No Arrest."), contradiction ("She Never Left."), system failure ("Police Had His DNA. In 1986.")
- Cold teal/charcoal + one red accent — matches Criminally Drawn brand

**Retention mechanics:**
- Multi-case compilations and iceberg tiers reward long sessions — different format from Criminally Drawn's single-case documentary
- Their **script cold-opens are often slow** (birth date, geography). Hooks live in **title + thumbnail + chapter stakes**, not always line one
- Criminally Drawn should do **both**: DLAM-level title curiosity + **stronger cold open** than DLAM (our differentiator)

### Do NOT copy

- Profanity-bait titles ("f*cked up", "batsh*t") — off-brand for Criminally Drawn
- Reddit/4chan compilation format — we do one full case
- 40–60 min iceberg marathons — we target 25 min, complete arc
- "Trust me bro" / slang narrator voice — Jonty is dry, adult, documentary

## 3. Criminally Drawn hook rules (for script generation)

### Cold open (first 20–30 seconds) — MANDATORY structure

1. **Sentence 1:** Present-tense stake or contradiction (no birth dates, no "was born in...")
2. **Sentence 2–3:** Sharpen the paradox — what should have been impossible
3. **Sentence 4–5:** Time anchor + "and then..." forward pull
4. **Only after hook:** context / chronology

**Banned openings:**
- "[Name] was born on..."
- "In this video we will..."
- Wikipedia lead paragraph ("X was an American serial killer who...")
- Meta commentary about evidence as a "medium"

**Good hook shapes for Jonty:**
- **Investigator trap:** He sat in the room where detectives rebuilt his crimes.
- **Timeline break:** She was alive when the ransom note was written. That is the problem.
- **Institutional failure:** They had his DNA before his first murder conviction.
- **Last normal moment:** The call lasted eleven seconds. She hung up smiling.

### Retention cadence (25 min script)

- Micro-beat every **15–20 seconds**: new fact, date, location shift, or unanswered question
- **Act breaks** (~7 min): re-hook with a single-sentence stakes reset ("By 1987, three people were dead. Police still had no idea they were interviewing him.")
- No recap paragraphs between acts
- Clinical violence only — no gore, no child imagery

### Title ↔ thumbnail ↔ hook alignment

All three must promise the **same paradox**. If title says "He Sent the Clues," cold open must be about the letters, not childhood.

- **Title:** He Named Himself. Police Still Couldn't Find Him.

He had also mailed police a poem about one of his murders, signed with a name he invented himself: BTK. Bind, Torture, Kill. For thirty years, investigators chased a ghost who kept writing back. The terrifying part is not that he hid in plain sight. It is that he enjoyed the conversation."
